Early Life

I was born in College Station, Texas on September 29, 2005. After living in Korea for nearly the entirety of my parent's lives, they decided to move to the States in pursuit of my father's PhD in computer science. As a child of Asian immigrants, it was difficult for me to connect to the people around me since Korean culture barely had any presence in Texas at the time. This issue was quickly fixed, however, when my family moved to San Diego, California, when a job opportunity opened up for my father. Ever since then, we have been happily living in San Diego for over a decade.

The Beginnings of a Chargers Fan

When I was around nine years old, I watched Super Bowl 50 where Peyton Manning and Von Miller led the Denver Broncos to the Lombardi Trophy, winning 24-10 against the Carolina Panthers. Although nine-year-old me knew what American Football was, this was the first time I ever watched a real game. Instantly, I fell in love with the game and luckily, there was a team waiting for me to cheer for: The San Diego Chargers. I watched Philip Rivers lead his team to one of the top offenses in the league, but also sadly sexperienced the Chargers move to the City of Angels. Nowadays, I am still a proud Chargers fan where hopefully one day I will be able to see them lift the Lombardi Trophy.

Future

I would say that most people do not know what their future careers are going to be. I myself had the same problem where I first wanted to be a firefighter which then swapped to a doctor, and finally a psychiatrist. As of today, however, I have committed myself to follow in the footsteps of my father in becoming a software engineer. Although I was not a prodigy at coding nor disassembled machines as a child, the exponential growth of modern technology has drawn me into this field. I hope that one day I will be able to work for a game company, but after recent news of the horrible work environment in most video game companies, I have hesitation and might pursue a position in other tech giants such as Facebook or Apple.
